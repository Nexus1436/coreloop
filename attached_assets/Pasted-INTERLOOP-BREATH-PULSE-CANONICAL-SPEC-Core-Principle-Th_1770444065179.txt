INTERLOOP — BREATH / PULSE CANONICAL SPEC

Core Principle
The pulse represents breath and attention, not activity or status.
It must feel biological, inevitable, and non-mechanical.

⸻

Timing
	•	Total cycle: 6.5 seconds
	•	Inhale (expansion): ~3.8s
	•	Exhale (contraction): ~2.7s
	•	Easing: slow ease-in, long ease-out
	•	No sharp peaks, no snapping

⸻

Contraction (Exhale)
	•	The pulse contracts toward the center until it becomes nearly a point
	•	Minimum scale: 0.15 – 0.2
	•	Opacity during contraction:
	•	Starts very low
	•	Never exceeds 40%
	•	The center should feel empty, not solid
	•	No visible “ball” formation

If it ever looks like a dot, circle, or orb → it’s wrong.

⸻

Expansion (Inhale)
	•	The pulse expands through and beyond the logo
	•	Maximum scale: 1.3 – 1.5× the logo diameter
	•	As it expands:
	•	Opacity decreases, not increases
	•	Edges become diffuse
	•	The pulse should feel like pressure dissipating
	•	By the time it reaches logo edges:
	•	Opacity should be < 25%
	•	Blur should be increased
	•	Past the logo:
	•	Pulse fades naturally into black
	•	No hard boundary

⸻

Opacity Curve (Critical)
	•	Inverse relationship to size
	•	Small = slightly visible
	•	Large = barely visible

Absolutely no moment where:
	•	Large = opaque
	•	Small = solid

⸻

Blur / Softness
	•	Blur increases with expansion
	•	Blur decreases with contraction
	•	Blur should never fully disappear
	•	No glow, shimmer, halo, or neon effects

⸻

Color
	•	Pulse color: charcoal / graphite
	•	Base: rgba(58, 58, 58)
	•	Never pure white
	•	Never high contrast

⸻

Logo Layer
	•	Logo is static
	•	Color: charcoal
	•	Opacity: ~85–90%
	•	Position: centered
	•	The pulse exists behind and through the logo
	•	The logo must never clip, mask, or suppress the pulse

If removing the logo causes the pulse to change behavior → layering is wrong.

⸻

Intro Text (“I hear you.”)
	•	Appears centered on first load
	•	Opacity: soft (≈50–60%)
	•	Text floats above both pulse and logo
	•	On first tap:
	•	Text fades out only
	•	Pulse and logo remain untouched
	•	The pulse must never reduce text legibility

⸻

Explicit Failure Conditions

If any of the following happen, the implementation is incorrect:
	•	The pulse looks like a circle
	•	The pulse gets more opaque as it expands
	•	The pulse stops at the logo edge
	•	The center ever looks filled or solid
	•	The logo masks or clips the pulse
	•	The pulse disappears when text fades
